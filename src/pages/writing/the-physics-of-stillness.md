You may think you’re sitting still.

But even as you read this, you’re traveling through space at extraordinary speed.

• The Earth spins on its axis at about 1,670 km/h (1,040 mph).  
• That same Earth orbits the Sun at roughly 107,000 km/h (66,500 mph).  
• The Sun, in turn, races around the center of the Milky Way at nearly 828,000 km/h (514,000 mph).  
• Our entire galaxy drifts through the cosmic web toward the Virgo Cluster at more than 2.1 million km/h (1.3 million mph). At the same time, space itself stretches, carrying us ever farther from everything else.

Add it all together, and every “motionless” person is streaking through the universe at several million kilometers per hour.

Yet you don’t feel it.

The room seems still, the air calm, your heartbeat steady.

The reason is equilibrium. Every force acting upon you is matched by another.

Spin, orbit, drift, and expansion remain perfectly balanced in their geometry of motion.

So when you sit still, you’re actually participating in the universe’s most elegant act:

absolute motion held in perfect equilibrium.

Stillness isn’t the absence of motion.  
It’s motion so harmonized you can finally feel the quiet.
