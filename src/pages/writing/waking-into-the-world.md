Each morning begins with a small miracle.

While I sleep, the world rearranges itself. Light softens, streets rinse clean, and the air rehearses its first quiet breath. When I open my eyes, it’s not the same world I left behind. It’s one that’s been renewed, as if nature itself pressed save and started a new version overnight.

I’ve come to love that moment—the slow rise from pillow to window, when the mind decides whether to rejoin the living. Some mornings I’m ready to sprint. Others, I linger, sifting through the weight of unfinished things, deciding what still matters enough to carry forward. Not everything deserves precious bandwidth.

A sunrise isn’t an invitation to start over. It’s a reminder to continue with intention—to let the right things follow us into the light and allow the rest to dissolve quietly with the dark.

Agency, defined.

Every day, I get to choose again.  
Every day, the world meets me new.

Walk with me awhile, and maybe we’ll both remember what’s worth keeping.
