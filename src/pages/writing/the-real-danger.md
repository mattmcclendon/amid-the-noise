The real danger  
is not that lies exist.

The real danger  
is that we hear so many lies  
they become ambient.

They stop reading as false  
and start passing as normal.

When lies are constant,  
truth does not lose because it is weaker.  
It loses because it no longer feels distinct.

A nervous system cannot remain alert forever.  
A mind flooded with contradictions adapts by flattening them.

Urgency blurs into background noise.  
Warning becomes wallpaper.

At that point, truth is not rejected.  
It is simply unrecognizable.

This is how collapse happens.  
Not through a single deception,  
but through saturation.

Not through silence,  
but through relentless distortion.

When everything is framed as a threat,  
discernment shuts down.

When every claim demands belief,  
belief itself loses meaning.

The real danger  
is not being lied to.

The real danger  
is forgetting what truth sounds like.
