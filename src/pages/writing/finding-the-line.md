Sundays have a way of revealing direction.

Not all at once. More like a line that comes into focus when everything else quiets down.

I used to think direction meant certainty. A plan. A destination.

It’s simpler than that.

Direction is alignment.

It shows up when what you’re doing stops arguing with who you are. When the next step doesn’t need a reason. It just needs to be taken.

You don’t find it by searching harder. You find it by noticing what holds.

What you return to without being told.  
What sharpens you instead of draining you.

That’s the line.

Once you see it, you stop negotiating with yourself. You move.

My Sunday reflection:

Direction isn’t something you decide once. It’s something you recognize, then choose again.
