# Amid the Noise
## Archive Illustration System

This document defines the recurring visual language used throughout Amid the Noise.

Every Archive Plate should feel as though it belongs to the same long-forgotten museum collection documenting curiosity, learning, exploration, systems, and memory. No single plate tells the whole story. Together they form the visual archive of Amid the Noise.

---

# Master Style Guide

Every illustration in the Amid the Noise archive should feel as though it belongs to the same museum collection. The individual subject changes, but the visual language never does.

## Purpose

These illustrations are not concept art, marketing graphics, or decorative filler. They are archival plates that create moments of reflection throughout the site. Every piece should feel timeless, meticulously observed, and quietly human.

## Canvas

- Default size: **1920 × 270 pixels** (64:9 panoramic divider)
- Treat every 1920 × 270 illustration as a panoramic museum plate with protected margins.
- Keep all essential visual content inside a safe composition area of approximately **1600 × 170 pixels**, centered within the canvas.
- Maintain minimum gutters of **160 px** on the left and right and **50 px** on the top and bottom.
- No significant object, shadow, annotation, accession number, or linework should approach the canvas edge.
- The surrounding empty paper is considered part of the composition, not unused space.
- Hero illustrations may use **1920 × 1080** when specified.
- Background color: **#f8f7f4**.
- No borders.
- No frames.
- No vignette.
- No visible paper edge.

## Composition

- Leave generous negative space.
- The primary subject must remain completely inside the safe composition area.
- For panoramic plates, favor low, horizontal compositions over tall vertical arrangements.
- If a subject is naturally tall, rotate it diagonally, reduce its scale, or lower the viewing angle so it fits comfortably within the protected margins.
- Never crop the primary subject unless the prompt explicitly requests a close study.
- Allow the subject to occupy roughly 30–60% of the canvas depending on scale.
- Avoid perfect symmetry.
- The illustration should feel discovered rather than staged.
- Favor calm, museum-quality compositions over dramatic ones.

## Illustration Style

Render every subject as a hand-drawn archival ink plate.

Use:

- fine black technical pen
- nineteenth-century scientific engraving
- architectural drafting precision
- natural variation in line weight
- dense crosshatching
- fine stippling
- restrained contour lines
- meticulous mechanical detail

The illustration should reward close inspection.

## Lighting

Suggest light through preserved paper and ink density.

Never use:

- gradients
- glow effects
- bloom
- blur
- painterly shading

White is simply untouched paper.

## Background

Backgrounds should remain quiet.

Only include environmental detail when it supports the composition.

Large areas of empty paper are encouraged.

## Archive Catalog

Every illustration should include one subtle archival accession number.

Examples:

- ATN-EXC-001
- ATN-PEN-001
- ATN-BKS-001

Treat these like museum catalog numbers rather than titles.

## Negative Prompt

Do not generate:

- photorealism
- watercolor
- charcoal
- comic-book styling
- anime or manga
- digital painting
- glossy rendering
- modern UI graphics
- lens flares
- motion blur
- heavy textures
- borders
- frames
- watermarks
- large blocks of readable text
- cropped primary subjects
- objects, shadows, or linework touching the top, bottom, left, or right edge of the canvas

## Overall Intent

Every illustration should feel like a page removed from a forgotten archive documenting curiosity, learning, systems, exploration, and memory. Individually they are quiet artifacts. Together they become the visual language of Amid the Noise.
